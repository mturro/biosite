I feel like I've been here before. This moment, this 'disruption', this automated intrusion into the daily life and practice of a skilled workforce is familiar. Yet the echo of that last time, where the human toil was outpaced by bits of work expressed via silicon, resonates only in the sense that something, some craft, is being sidelined. Not the craft of exposing film or setting type or etching plates, but still a craft. This moment is odd. The same in that there are people who have practiced this craft for years being upended, having their professional identity challenged by the sudden onset of technical change. But different in that this technical change is recursive and born of the world it is so completely breaking down.
